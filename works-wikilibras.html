Wikilibras

The product

It is a crowdsourcing Platform for building a dictionary of the Brazilian Sign Language (LIBRAS) using specifying gestures for a 3D avatar.

Key words

Deafs and Hard of hearing, Accessibility, Technology, Inclusion

Concept

Breaking stereotypes getting away from the topic "hearing impaired", keep the iconic representation of Sign Language, use young people engaged to the deaf community as a target.

Background

I was the designer in charge to develop the visual identity and the UI and UX of WikiLIBRAS. WikiLIBRAS came from VLIBRAS that is an app for Brazilian Sign Language communication. In VLIBRAS the user writes down a text and the app translates as video of an avatar communicating in sign language. Languages are always modifying, it is an on-going process. WikiLIBRAS was created as a crowdsourcing dictionary where people can contribute by adding new words to VLIVRAS.

Process

1 Context

Through interviews and brainstormings, we created a panel with images and words that reflect "deafness". We observed that the society express deafness in an institutional and sad way relating it to disability. Also, we observed the tendency to use a representation of hands as a symbol.

2 Target

The Wikilibras user is a young deaf/heard of hearing person who uses the internet as a tool to break social exclusion. She/He uses the app thinking about a future in which the deaf/heard of hearing won’t be seen as a person with physical deficiency and the sign language won’t be a limitation to communication. She/He considers the WikiLibras as a way to this future. Wishing to help with this, this persona is engaged on WikiLibras always looking for new users. This persona enjoys to use the WikiLibras T-shirt, not just because of what it represents but also because she/he likes the logo colors and shapes. Being deaf is not who she/he is, it is just a characteristic that she/he has as a unique person, that’s why she/he likes to raise curiosity in people who see her/him wearing the WikiLibras T-shirt as opposed to the usual and obvious deaf campaigns related T-shirts.
